Then And Now.

A place beyond, A place I wasn't so fond of. The air felt new, my heart renewed, The nasty holes that had been eaten through, By fear, doubt and worry, though reasons were few. I had left my home, My mother land, To a new place called England. I never knew I'd be here, In a place where, There's be so much, That I felt rushed. I felt inferior, I felt different... I wasn't inferior, I was different. And now. I was different too, Not on the outside, On the inside and through. But you learnt to adapt, And suddenly it wasn't so bad. It was time to write a new chapter, And turn a new page. And now in a new age, I would leave my cage, And spread my wings, And fly into the sky, to learn new things
