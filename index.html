Black? British? Both?

Black, British, or both? I guess I will never know… Being half is not enough, And it will never be that much. Why Why Why can’t I be like everyone else, ‘Where are you from?’ is a bad question ‘What race are you?’ is even worse, Answering those will be like hell. Why Why Why can’t I just be one? If you ask; ‘Am I black?’ I’ll say no, If you ask; ‘Am I white?’, it’ll be the same Why Why Why is it so complicated? Is it that I’m cursed? Or am I blessed? Just having to pick one is hard. Why Why Why Why can’t I be like everyone else? To know that there was one place I truly belonged To be able to find peace, not be discriminated or judged Is a miracle for people like me. Why Why Why can’t I look the same, Hair, Skin and face, Why one big long name? I feel like a string being wound up along the finger. Why Why Why can’t I be like everyone else, ‘Where are you from?’ is a bad question ‘What race are you?’ is even worse, Answering those will be like hell. Why Why Black, British, or both? I guess I will never know… Being half is not enough, And it will never be that much.

My History Is My Pride

Home isn’t just where you live. It is your story. It is your history. And not everyone has the courage, To step up for what is right. We talk about it, We complain. But We all know something is wrong. Just most of us know what is wrong. Only some of us know what to do. Merely a few of us know how to do it. But only certain ones of us… Actually take a step. But Only a few undeniable souls. ‘Say’ that something should be done, And then ‘do’ it. Only a few undeniable souls… Change the world. But It’s not for themselves, Or the current generation, But for the future ones. The ones that can change tomorrow. But only if they try. But We need those certain ones. Those undeniable souls. And that can be you. But Only if you choose so.

Then And Now

A place beyond, A place I wasn't so fond of. The air felt new, my heart renewed, The nasty holes that had been eaten through, By fear, doubt and worry, though reasons were few. I had left my home, My mother land, To a new place called England. I never knew I'd be here, In a place where, There's be so much, That I felt rushed. I felt inferior I felt different... I wasn't inferior I was different. And now. I was different too, Not on the outside, On the inside and through. But you learnt to adapt And suddenly it wasn't so bad. It was time to write a new chapter. And turn a new page. And now in a new age, I would leave my cage, And spread my wings And fly into the sky, to learn new things

Beneath The Iron Sky

Mama once said ‘if you are smart you are strong’. I was smart, I was strong. There was a day, The birds did not sing their morning song. I took a walk. And met a stranger on the way. A tall dark figure, With no face or name, But hollow holes for eyes, Hollow bottomless pits. But when it spoke, it’s voice, Was not only one voice, But a series of screams, Crushed under a growl, That felt like a dying fowl. The figure asked me my name. It seemed tame, But had an aura so poisonous, That a bird nearby dropped from It’s tree. So i replied, with my most charming smile- ‘Onwumbiko.’ Meaning "Death, I implore you" The figure's mouth twisted into a sneer, ‘Why?’ he growled, and i grinned, ‘I shall make you a throne. Someone as great as you shouldn’t have to stand while you make me suffer. I will create a throne befitting your shadow.’ I reply The figure smiles condescending. So I went away, And built the throne i promised, The back of the chair made of iron, The seat was covered with a thin cloth, Crimson red– the colour of blood And as the figure sat, The cloth gave way, The figure fell down into an abyss, Which truly was a well, Deep to the core of the earth, Into eternal fire he fell, Never seen again. I became a hero, Of my own telling, And that is how Onwumbiko, Cheated. The. One. Who. Cheated. Others.

We Know It Came

When the leaves fall They leave the trees barren, When the time has come, We leave and let them imagine, The days go by without a doubt The hours fly past, no sign of devout. The snow comes, Cold and biting, and scorching your lungs Then it’s gone, Now we’re gone, We were once here, Now, never again. Then spring will come. And children would dance for joy. Villagers sing songs of praise, For what has begun, a new phase is born. All defrosts, and comes to life, And now, drawn on all, is a new design. Now it stays, but not for long. Every morning the birds will sing a morning song, Even if it goes, We know it came. And for me, That’s all that matters in this game.
